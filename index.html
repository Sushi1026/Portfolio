Here in the Games section, there are my personal favorite Godot games! In easier terms, Godot games are simply games that are created and/or designed in the Godot program! I decided to attach them because they provide a form of nostalgia and fun for whoever plays them. With bright colors, yummy pizza, and leafy mazes, players can challenges themselves and look at the code. For example, there is a "2D Snek Game" and a "2D Brick-Breaker Game" I have made. In the brick-breaker game, you can control a paddle with a ball to break all the brick obstacles in your way. To code this, I used the script and the Godot coding language to give the separate "nodes" commands. As these being some of my favorite Godot games, I hope you enjoy them as much as I did!

Arduino

Home | Games | Websites

Arduino is truly something to marvel at! With the use of a circuit board, resistors, and other parts, people can make traffic lights, digital dice, and more. These Arduino projects I worked on took up a lot of time and effort, but in the end, the result was worth it. Projects like this also can display one's logical reasoning, patience, and focus. The arduino project shown on the side is the "Traffic Light" project. The traffic lights correspond to signal when cars go, slow down, and stop, while also displaying when pedestrians can stop and go. In the picture, it doesn't depict the final product, in which there are more resistors and lines for each LED so it could light up when commanded. This works by connecting the breadboard, which is shown in the image, to the circuit board, which is connected to the device. With this setup, I used pieces like the button and LEDs and put them in places on the breadboard, connecting the line to the circuit board. In the code, I corresponded the values the LED are placed at so they could run smoothly. Another example, not depicted, would be the "LED Dice." The dice lights up a random amount of LEDs to represent a random number you roll on a dice. Arduino is really amazing!
